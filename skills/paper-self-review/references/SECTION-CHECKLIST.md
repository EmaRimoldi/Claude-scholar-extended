# Section Checklist

## Abstract
- problem clear
- method identifiable
- main result quantified
- contribution stated

## Introduction
- research gap explicit
- question or hypothesis visible
- contribution list concrete

## Method
- enough detail to reproduce
- assumptions named
- key design choices justified

## Results
- numbers support claims
- uncertainty / significance reported when needed
- baselines and ablations are interpretable

## Discussion
- limitations named
- failure cases acknowledged
- next-step implications are reasonable
- alternative explanations discussed for primary results
- "what would falsify this?" paragraph present
- causal language used only when causation is demonstrated (not just correlation)

## Claim-Conclusion Audit (after full draft)
- every claim in Abstract, Introduction contributions, and Conclusion has a corresponding entry in claim-evidence-map.md
- no claim in Discussion or Conclusion is stated more strongly than its claim-evidence-map rating warrants
- the title does not convey stronger certainty than the weakest primary claim's evidence level
- the abstract's key result sentence uses the same hedging level as recommended by the claim-evidence map
- negative or null results from the analysis bundle are mentioned in Discussion (not silently dropped)
- "state-of-the-art" is claimed only if all relevant baselines in the field were compared
- contribution bullets in Introduction match the actual results, not the originally planned contributions
